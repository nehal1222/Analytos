# GroundTruth — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

This document defines the ICP segments and personas for both GroundTruth
products, Stockly and Inspectly, and the market signals GTM should watch for.

## Segment: Mid-Market Retail & E-commerce (Stockly)

- **Firmographics:** $50M–$800M annual revenue, 50–2,000 retail locations
  and/or a direct-to-consumer e-commerce operation doing at least $20M/year
  online, 200–3,000 employees.
- **Tech stack signals:** Running a legacy or homegrown forecasting process
  (spreadsheets, or an aging planning tool like **ForecastPro Classic**),
  on Shopify Plus / BigCommerce / a mid-market ERP (NetSuite, Microsoft
  Dynamics 365 Business Central).
- **Primary persona:** **VP of Supply Chain** (economic buyer) — owns
  inventory investment, judged on carrying cost and stockout rate.
- **Secondary persona:** **Inventory Planning Manager** (champion / daily
  user) — the person actually running replenishment cycles today, usually
  in spreadsheets or a dated planning tool.
- **Trigger signals:**
  - Recent Series C+ funding round or PE recapitalization (budget for new
    tooling just freed up).
  - Job posting for "Demand Planner" or "Inventory Planning Manager" (signals
    the function is under-resourced or scaling).
  - Public statements about opening 20+ new stores or launching new sales
    channels in the next 12 months.
  - ERP migration announced or in progress (NetSuite or Dynamics 365
    implementation) — forecasting tooling gets re-evaluated in the same
    cycle.
- **Competitor displacement angle:** Incumbent tools like **ForecastPro
  Classic** and manual spreadsheet processes can't do SKU x location daily
  refresh or auto-replenishment; lead with the 62%→89% forecast accuracy
  jump and the 3.5-month payback period as the wedge.

## Segment: Regulated Manufacturing — Medical Devices (Inspectly)

- **Firmographics:** $100M–$1B annual revenue, FDA-registered manufacturing
  facility (Class II or III devices), ISO 13485 certified or pursuing
  certification, 500–5,000 employees.
- **Tech stack signals:** Manual or semi-manual visual inspection stations,
  paper or PDF-based audit documentation, legacy QMS software (e.g.
  **QualityWorks QMS**) that doesn't integrate with line equipment.
- **Primary persona:** **VP/Director of Quality Assurance** (economic buyer)
  — accountable to the FDA and to leadership for defect escape rate and
  audit outcomes.
- **Secondary persona:** **Compliance / Regulatory Affairs Manager**
  (champion) — owns audit prep and lives in dread of a Form 483 observation.
- **Trigger signals:**
  - A recent FDA Form 483 observation or warning letter (urgent, budget
    unlocks fast).
  - Upcoming scheduled FDA audit or ISO 13485 recertification within 6
    months.
  - New product line launch requiring new inspection stations (natural
    point to evaluate tooling instead of replicating the old manual
    process).
  - Job posting for "Quality Systems Engineer" or expansion of the QA team.
- **Competitor displacement angle:** Manual inspection and legacy QMS tools
  like **QualityWorks QMS** can't produce a continuous audit trail — lead
  with the "3 weeks → 2 days audit prep" proof point and the 1.8%→0.3%
  defect escape rate, since both map directly to what a QA Director gets
  measured on.

## Notes for GTM

- Always ground outreach in a proof point from the product graph, not a
  generic claim — evaluators and prospects alike will ask "where does that
  number come from."
- Do not reference specific pilot customer names in outbound prospecting
  material; use proof points and segment language only.
